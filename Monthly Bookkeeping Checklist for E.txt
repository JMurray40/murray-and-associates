Monthly Bookkeeping Checklist for Entrepreneurs

Staying on top of your finances doesn’t have to be overwhelming. With a simple monthly routine, you can keep your books accurate and your business on track.

Here’s a practical monthly bookkeeping checklist every entrepreneur should follow.

1. Review and Categorize Transactions

Each week (or at minimum, monthly):

Review all bank and credit card transactions
Assign correct categories
Confirm nothing is missing
2. Reconcile Bank and Credit Card Accounts

This step ensures your records match reality.

Compare QuickBooks balances to statements
Investigate any discrepancies
Confirm all accounts are reconciled
3. Review Accounts Receivable (A/R)
Check outstanding invoices
Follow up on overdue payments
Ensure customer balances are accurate

Cash flow depends on getting paid—don’t ignore this step.

4. Review Accounts Payable (A/P)
Confirm all bills are entered
Review upcoming payments
Avoid late fees or missed obligations
5. Analyze Your Financial Reports

Look beyond the numbers—understand them.

Review:

Profit & Loss: Are you making money?
Expenses: Are any categories too high?
Trends: Are things improving or declining?
6. Check Cash Flow

Cash is king.

Review current cash balance
Forecast upcoming expenses
Ensure you have enough runway
7. Document and Store Receipts
Attach receipts in QuickBooks or store digitally
Keep records organized for tax purposes
8. Adjust and Clean Up Entries
Record depreciation, loan payments, or accruals if needed
Fix miscoded transactions
Ensure accuracy before closing the month
Final Thought

Consistency is the key to clean books. By following this checklist each month, you’ll avoid surprises, reduce stress, and gain better control over your business finances.
